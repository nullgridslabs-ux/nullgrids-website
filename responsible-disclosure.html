RESPONSIBLE DISCLOSURE

LAST UPDATED: V1.0 // 2026
We Welcome Ethical Researchers

NULLGRIDS Labs is a cybersecurity organization. We respect and appreciate the security research community. If you discover a vulnerability in our platforms, please report it responsibly so we can fix it before it is exploited.

1. Scope

This policy applies to vulnerabilities discovered in NULLGRIDS Labs owned and operated services:

  • nullgridslabs.dpdns.org — Main organization website
  • blackcipher.nullgridslabs.dpdns.org — CTF competition platform

Third-party services, social media accounts, or infrastructure not directly operated by NULLGRIDS Labs are out of scope.

2. How to Report

1

Document your findings — Include vulnerability type, affected URL/endpoint, steps to reproduce, and potential impact. Screenshots or PoC code are welcome but not required.

2

Send an email to nullgridslabs@gmail.com with the subject line: [VULNERABILITY REPORT]

3

Allow us time to respond — We will acknowledge your report within 72 hours and aim to resolve confirmed vulnerabilities within 14 days depending on severity.

4

Coordinate disclosure — Please refrain from public disclosure until we have patched the issue and given you the green light, or until 30 days have passed since your initial report.

3. What We Ask of You

  • Do not access, modify, or delete data that is not yours.
  • Do not perform DoS/DDoS attacks against our infrastructure.
  • Do not exploit the vulnerability beyond what is necessary to demonstrate it.
  • Do not use social engineering or phishing tactics against NULLGRIDS team members.
  • Act in good faith and with the intent to improve security.

4. What We Offer

Researchers who responsibly disclose valid vulnerabilities will receive:

  • A written acknowledgement of receipt within 72 hours.
  • Credit in our public Hall of Thanks (with your permission).
  • A coordinated disclosure timeline that respects your research.

Note: NULLGRIDS Labs is a student-led non-profit initiative. We do not currently offer monetary bug bounties, but we are deeply grateful for ethical research contributions.

5. Legal Safe Harbor

NULLGRIDS Labs will not pursue legal action against researchers who discover and report vulnerabilities in good faith in accordance with this policy. We consider good-faith security research conducted under this policy to be authorized and beneficial.
